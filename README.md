# nyc_airbnb_tableau
Interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing NYC Airbnb pricing, room types, and neighborhood trends across 5,000 listings.
Overview

This project presents an interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing the NYC Airbnb market across 5,000 listings. The dashboard examines pricing by borough, room type distribution, the most reviewed neighbourhoods, and the geographic spread of listings across New York City — the kind of analysis a real estate analyst or hospitality consultant would deliver to inform market strategy.

Dataset


Source: New York City Airbnb Open Data — Kaggle
Coverage: 5,000 NYC Airbnb listings
Tool: Tableau Public


Dashboard Components

Chart 1 — Average Price by NYC Borough

Manhattan leads all boroughs at $200+ average price per night, nearly double Brooklyn's average. This confirms Manhattan's premium positioning in the short-term rental market.

Chart 2 — Listings by Room Type

Entire home/apartment listings dominate with nearly 3,000 listings, more than double private rooms. Shared rooms represent a negligible share of the market, suggesting guests strongly prefer privacy.

Chart 3 — Top 10 Most Reviewed Neighbourhoods

Williamsburg leads all neighbourhoods with over 30,000 total reviews, followed by Bedford-Stuyvesant and Harlem. Brooklyn neighbourhoods make up the majority of the top 10, reflecting strong demand outside Manhattan.

Chart 4 — Average Price by Room Type

Entire home/apt averages $220+ per night compared to $100 for private and shared rooms. Hosts seeking maximum revenue should prioritize listing entire units over individual rooms.

Chart 5 — Geographic Map by Borough

The map visualizes listing concentration across NYC's five boroughs, with bubble size representing total reviews. Manhattan and Brooklyn dominate in both volume and engagement.

Key Insights


Manhattan commands a significant price premium — hosts and investors should prioritize Manhattan properties for maximum nightly revenue
Brooklyn is the most reviewed market despite lower average prices, indicating high volume demand and strong guest activity
Entire home listings generate over twice the revenue of private rooms — hosts should consider converting private room listings to full unit rentals where possible
Williamsburg and Bedford-Stuyvesant are the highest-traffic neighbourhoods, making them prime targets for new Airbnb investments
